Game Instructions

1 Understand the Challenge

The Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP) is a classic computer science problem: Find the shortest possible route that visits each city exactly once and returns to the origin city.

This problem is famously difficult (NP-hard), meaning that as the number of cities increases, the number of possible routes grows exponentially. For just 10 cities, there are over 3 million possible routes!

2 Choose Your Difficulty

Select a difficulty level from the dropdown menu:

  • Easy: 10 cities - Good for beginners
  • Medium: 15 cities - Moderate challenge
  • Hard: 25 cities - Difficult optimization
  • Expert: 40 cities - Very challenging
  • Custom: Set your own number of cities (5-100)

Then click the "New Game" button to generate a random set of cities.

Tip:

For mobile devices, we recommend starting with fewer cities (10-20) for better performance and touch accuracy.

3 Create Your Path

Click on cities one by one to create your path. Your goal is to:

  • Visit each city exactly once
  • Return to your starting city to complete the tour
  • Find the shortest possible total distance

The first city you click becomes your starting point (green). As you build your path, visited cities turn blue, and unvisited cities remain red.

Tip:

Carefully consider your starting city - it can significantly impact your final path length!

4 Complete Your Tour

After you've visited all cities (except one), clicking on the final unvisited city will automatically complete your tour by returning to your starting point.

The game will display your total path length and compare it to the algorithm solutions.

6 Game Controls

Use these controls to aid your gameplay:

  • Undo Last: Remove the last city from your path
  • Reset Path: Clear your current path and start over
  • Clear Best: Clear your best recorded path length
  • City Size: Adjust the size of cities for better visibility
  • Algorithm Buttons: View solutions from each algorithm

Why TSP Matters

The Traveling Salesperson Problem isn't just a game - it has important real-world applications:

  • Logistics and Route Planning: Delivery services, postal routes, and transportation networks
  • Circuit Board Manufacturing: Optimizing drilling operations
  • DNA Sequencing: Arranging DNA fragments
  • Computer Wiring: Minimizing cable lengths
  • Warehouse Order Picking: Optimizing paths through warehouses

By playing this game, you're tackling one of the most studied problems in computational complexity!
